### Area 1 / Decision 1 — lifecycle classification

The operator ratified that every operational lifecycle action attempt is
observation-bearing: install, uninstall, supervisor start, stop, restart,
upgrade, schema migration, backup, repair, reconciliation, and shutdown. This
includes failed attempts and successful no-op reconciliation. Status and
readiness inspection remain projection-only.

Two riders were ratified:

- Ledger retention, archival, and export are reserved future actions, and any
  mutation of the observation store is pre-classified as observation-bearing.
- This is classification, not bootstrap or ledger-failure ordering. Install is
  still subject to the bootstrap decision and shutdown to the failure-edge
  decision. An unclean termination cannot be observed at crash time; the next
  start reconstructs it through an observed reconciliation.

### Area 1 / Decision 2 — bootstrap ordering

The operator ratified the six-point bootstrap ordering. Bootstrap may create
only the minimal substrate needed to acquire the first exclusive ledger writer.
That substrate grants no authority and establishes neither completed install
nor readiness. The first writer records the authenticated bootstrap initiator,
intended lifecycle action, and discovered pre-existing state before remaining
install/first-start effects. Identity/config persistence, service registration,
endpoint binding, and readiness follow the durable bootstrap fact. Append
failure suppresses remaining protected effects and success acknowledgement;
minimal residue is reconciled on the next attempt. Readiness requires a durable
bootstrap chain.

The external-receipt alternative was rejected as an evidence channel. Such a
receipt may be input included in discovered-pre-existing-state content, but is
never authority or a parallel evidence format.

Two riders were ratified:

- Before resident identity exists, the authenticated bootstrap initiator is the
  OS principal (UID) performing the lifecycle action, using the D1.1 local-
  principal shape.
- Exclusive ledger-writer acquisition is the single-bootstrap admission gate.
  Exactly one attempt proceeds to first append; concurrent attempts fail closed
  without effects under the offline writer-lock precedent.

### Area 1 / Decision 3 — post-bootstrap writer failure

The operator ratified all eight proposed failure-edge points. Writer loss
fences Mother from readiness, new work, authority mutations, lifecycle work,
and unrelated cleanup. Work that has not begun another protected effect cannot
cross that effect boundary after loss is known.

Shutdown may terminate as a safety carve-out without fabricating an observed
successful shutdown; the next start reconciles the discontinuity. Explicit
observer restoration is the other carve-out: an authenticated local OS
principal holding exclusive ledger ownership may perform only the minimum work
to restore append capability. Prior evidence is preserved, recovery is
recorded before readiness or ordinary effects resume, failed recovery remains
fenced, and no emergency side journal becomes authority.

Two riders were ratified:

- Recovery facts or linked continuations bind the exact preserved prior-ledger
  bytes by digest. Preservation is immutable by reference, not filesystem
  convention, so recovery can be distinguished from disguised tampering.
- A protected effect already executing when writer loss is detected may reach
  its natural end, but its outcome is neither acknowledged nor cached for later
  delivery. Recovery/start reconciliation records the resulting discontinuity.

### Area 1 / Decision 4 — operational action roles

The operator ratified subject, initiator, and executor as distinct current
projections derived from authenticated records and action context. The subject
is the installation/node or operational resource being changed. The initiator
is the current authority or provenance that caused the action. The executor is
the installer, supervisor, resident, or adapter performing the attempt. Causal
delegation retains every applicable role; client-authored labels select none.
Lifecycle actions do not become `MctCall` callers.

For a supervisor-initiated action, the initiator is specifically the governing
supervisor record. That record carries the authenticated operator principal
that installed it. Authentication occurs during the observed policy-install
action; boot-time actions inherit provenance through the current record and
consistent process context. The ledger must not claim boot-time authentication.
Before node identity exists, bootstrap uses pending local installation scope as
subject and the authenticated OS UID as initiator.

The operator declared Area 1 complete. Under the briefing's explicit default,
existing observation kinds are reused: lifecycle, operator, adapter-effect, and
storage kinds compose the facts above; Area 1 adds no kernel observation kind.

### Area 2 / Decision 1 — acquisition source authority

The operator ratified two explicit acquisition paths: a standing current
operator-issued, scoped, revocable, time-bounded artifact-source authority
record, or one durably observed artifact-specific operator-pointed acquisition
that creates no ambient source trust. Source records identify source,
artifact/publisher/namespace scope, actions, additive integrity/provenance
policy, issuer, policy revision, state, and time bounds. Connectivity,
discovery, configuration, manifest reference, and source-access capability do
not establish trust. Both paths require current adapter effect authority and
neither grants installation, approval, assignment, or execution authority.

The integrity floor cannot be waived. Source policy may only add requirements;
operator-pointed decisions bind an expected digest when known. Independent byte
verification, not source trust, gates creation of an artifact record. Scope is
explicit: absent scope grants nothing; deliberately broad scope is valid only
when stated in the authority record.

### Area 2 / Decision 2 — acquisition as fifth lifecycle fact

The operator ratified immutable `ArtifactAcquisition` as the fifth independent
artifact/Child lifecycle fact. It records one attempted acquisition's standing
source or operator-pointed authority, adapter effect authority, claimed
artifact/source identity, observed size/digest, verification decision/outcome,
and observations. Acquisition stages bytes but does not install or publish an
artifact. Verification alone gates `ComponentArtifact`; installation,
approval, assignment, and instance creation remain separately observed facts.
Failed attempts remain evidence without creating an artifact, while repeated
acquisition may refer to one immutable artifact.

Uniform provenance is mandatory after this law: every new `ComponentArtifact`
references at least one `ArtifactAcquisition`. Local operator staging is an
operator-pointed filesystem acquisition, not an exemption. Predating artifacts
are reconciled or explicitly marked historical provenance-unknown and are not
silently represented as equivalent to acquisition-backed artifacts.

### Area 2 / Decision 3 — update authority

The operator ratified strict per-artifact approval for updates without
amendment. Trusted source automation may discover, acquire, verify, and stage,
but only explicit approval naming the exact content digest confers use.
Approval never transfers through child name, version, publisher, signing key,
source, or channel. Assignments remain pinned until normal explicit replacement;
revocation of an old generation cannot approve a replacement. Any future
pre-authorized update channel is a new authority shape requiring elicitation.

Non-normative guidance: approval tooling should surface acquisition and
verification evidence at decision time so exact-artifact approval is
provenance-informed in practice.

### Area 2 / Decision 4 — registry-sync quarry disposition

The operator ratified all eight proposed placements for `patinaMother` registry
source sync. Generic catalog/sync/reconciliation/staging becomes MCT product
responsibility; external source access becomes Toy adapters; source authority,
effect authority, integrity, and exact-artifact approval remain kernel law;
acquisition and verification are ledger evidence; Patina-specific source/belief
meaning stays in a Child; browsing/explanation/diagnostics are optional product
tooling; ambient trust, automatic lifecycle authority, allow-by-default access,
and inherited storage/API shape are rejected; recurring/event initiation waits
for Area 3 scheduling law.

Authenticated-source credentials remain governed independently by existing
secrets/connection authority. They are neither stored in nor implied by source
authority. Source trust and credential authority meet only at the acquisition
adapter.

### Area 3 / Decision 1 — standing call-trigger authority

The operator ratified `CallTriggerAuthority` as explicit standing authority.
Only an authenticated local principal authorized by current policy may create,
revise, or revoke it. Its explicit scope binds Mother, Vision, canonical caller,
WIT target, constrained payload/template, trigger class/source, validity,
issuer, record revision, and policy revision; missing scope grants nothing and
activation follows durable authority evidence. A Child may request but cannot
activate it.

Each firing creates a fresh immutable call and re-enters current integrity,
idempotency, authority, routing, revalidation, and effect law. Trigger authority
grants none of those downstream authorities and cannot preserve stale authority.
Firing evidence references the exact governing trigger record and policy
revision.

Call-origin mapping remains explicitly open for the implementing SPEC's
operator gate under D1.3. A firing must not default to `JvmAdapter` or another
existing origin whose lineage it does not truthfully have.

The operator then stopped elicitation. Remaining Area 3 decisions are named
open questions for continuation after implementation of the ratified work.

## Ratified decisions

1. Operational lifecycle attempts are observation-bearing; status/readiness
   reads are projections.
2. Future observation-store mutation is observation-bearing before design.
3. Unclean termination is reconstructed by the next start's reconciliation
   observation rather than fabricated as a crash-time observation.
4. Only minimal observer substrate may precede the first append; all remaining
   bootstrap effects and readiness require the durable bootstrap chain.
5. Pre-ledger receipts are untrusted discovered-state input, not evidence or
   authority.
6. Bootstrap authenticates the initiating OS UID and admits exactly one attempt
   through exclusive ledger-writer ownership.
7. Post-bootstrap writer loss fences Mother; only termination for safety and
   explicit minimal observer restoration are permitted carve-outs.
8. Recovery is operator-requested, exclusively owned, digest-binds preserved
   prior evidence, and is durably recorded before readiness resumes.
9. An in-progress protected effect may finish after detected loss, but its
   outcome is not acknowledged or cached and is reconciled as discontinuity.
10. Self-action facts separate subject, initiator, and executor as current
    projections; lifecycle actions never become `MctCall` callers.
11. Supervisor initiation derives from the governing record and its install-time
    operator provenance, never a fabricated boot-time authentication event.
12. Existing observation kinds compose Area 1 facts; no new kernel kind is
    introduced.
13. Artifact acquisition requires either current standing source authority or
    one observed artifact-specific operator-pointed decision.
14. Source trust, adapter effect authority, and artifact byte verification are
    independent gates; none grants lifecycle or execution authority.
15. Integrity policy is additive above the verification floor, and source scope
    must be explicit because absent scope grants nothing.
16. `ArtifactAcquisition` is the fifth independent lifecycle fact; acquisition,
    verified artifact/catalog publication, approval, assignment, and live
    instance remain separate.
17. Every post-law artifact has acquisition provenance. Local staging uses the
    same operator-pointed path; historical unknown provenance is explicit.
18. Updates retain strict exact-artifact approval; source/channel similarity,
    old-generation revocation, and automated staging confer no use authority.
19. The eight-part `patinaMother` registry-sync quarry disposition is ratified;
    useful mechanism is rebuilt while ambient authority and source shape are
    rejected.
20. Source trust and source credentials are independent authorities that meet
    only at the acquisition adapter.
21. `CallTriggerAuthority` is explicit, scoped, revocable standing permission
    to originate fresh call attempts; management requires current authenticated
    local authority and Child requests never grant it.
22. Every firing re-enters current call law and carries exact trigger-record and
    policy-revision provenance.
23. Trigger-fired `CallOrigin` is unresolved and cannot default to an existing
    untruthful lineage; the implementing SPEC requires an operator gate.
