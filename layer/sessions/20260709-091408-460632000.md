**Key decisions:**

- The resident UDS owner performs live mutation; direct offline mutation requires exclusive ledger ownership. Connected resident rejection never falls back to offline mutation.
- Replacement readiness precedes predecessor drain. SQLite transaction commit is the child-generation swap's point of no return, making a durable zero-ready replacement state unrepresentable.

- Per-hop vouching is permanent for `mct/call/0`: the executor authorizes the ImmediateCaller, while call/trace identifiers—not upstream identity claims—correlate sovereign ledgers.
- Derived peer routing requires two independently issued directional admissions, a fresh publication, Vision agreement, and ticket reachability. Either sovereign's revocation removes candidacy on the next evaluation.
