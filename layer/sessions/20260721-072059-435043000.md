### Area 3 continuation / Decision 1 — truthful trigger-firing origin

-- Decision: 2026-07-21 the operator ratified `CallOrigin::TriggerFiring` as the
single warranted kernel ontology addition for both temporal and event firings.
Trigger class and source remain explicit firing evidence under the prior R1;
they do not multiply the closed origin enum. Serialization is additive over
existing ledgers: historical origin spellings and facts are not rewritten.

Rider R1: trigger-firing idempotency scope includes the exact governing trigger
record id. Every distinct firing occurrence carries a distinct idempotency key;
retrying one occurrence reuses its key and may replay, while distinct
occurrences and distinct trigger records never share replay state. Key
derivation is SPEC-local; record-and-occurrence scoping is product law.

Rider R2: `TriggerFiring` is a local origin for routing and may source one remote
candidate under normal current bilateral authority. It is never a forwarded
arrival. Forwarding still changes ingress lineage to `Iroh`, preserving the

### Area 3 continuation / Decision 2B.1 — minimum watch scope

Rider R2: every delivered event or batch references the exact governing scope

### Area 3 continuation / Decision 2B.3 — delivery and evidence

`mctMother`-side source adapter durably records receipt and eligibility, then

`TriggerFiring` call. One event cannot use both paths.

Child-path evidence names the actual causative call. When that call was
trigger-fired it additionally links the exact firing, trigger record, and
revision; manual or other calls receive no fabricated trigger lineage. The
`mctMother` adapter path directly names the event and exact trigger record,
record revision, and policy revision.

Batch evidence records scope id/revision, monotonic sequence, observer authority,
source effect, and raw, eligible, coalesced, excluded, and capacity-refused
counts. Event evidence records stable event/batch identity, position, safe path
and mechanical facts, causal lineage, and compatibility validation. Delivery
evidence records the selected path, target, applicable authorities, planned or
resulting call, and durable result reference.

Receipt and delivery eligibility become durable before delivery or
acknowledgement; append failure suppresses delivery. An event is delivered only
when its target call has completed with a durable successful `MctResult`.

Rider R1: delivery-specific disposition is a pre-call closed set of `fired`,
`coalesced`, `suppressed`, and `capacity_refused`. Once a target call exists,

### Area 3 continuation / Decision 4A — missed-fire policy

-- Decision: 2026-07-21 every trigger record names `missed_fire_policy` as
`skip`, `coalesce_one`, or `fire_late_bounded`, with `skip` as the safe default.
Skip creates no late call and leaves typed evidence. Coalesce-one represents all
currently known misses with at most one fresh call and records the represented
count/range. Fire-late-bounded may create one fresh call per known occurrence in
original order under named SPEC-local per-record and resident-wide bounds;
excess occurrences become capacity-refused evidence.

Only known misses may be recovered. Temporal occurrences may be reconstructed
from the durable trigger record. Event occurrences require retained event
identity and sequence; a sequence gap without retained event content is loss
evidence, never permission to fabricate an event. Revocation, expiry,
supersession, narrowing, and all current call checks defeat catch-up regardless
of policy. Every catch-up call is a fresh `TriggerFiring`, and policy revision
cannot reinterpret misses governed by an older record revision.

Rider R1: catch-up evaluation is deterministic for one trigger record revision
and represented known-miss set. The coalesced identity or per-occurrence
identities derive from that set, so evaluation after a crash reproduces the
same occurrence and idempotency identities. Evaluation, crash, and
re-evaluation cannot fire one missed set twice.

### Area 3 continuation / Decision 4B — overlap policy

-- Decision: 2026-07-21 every trigger record names `overlap_policy` as `refuse`,
`coalesce_one`, or `queue_bounded`, with `refuse` as the safe default. Overlap
exists when the prior firing has a target call but no durable terminal
`MctResult`. Each trigger record has at most one active target call.

Refuse creates no call or pending state and records suppression naming the
active firing. Coalesce-one retains at most one deterministic pending
representative and keeps every constituent occurrence countable. Queue-bounded
assigns stable pending identities and deterministic order up to a named
SPEC-local per-record constant; excess occurrences become capacity-refused.
Dequeue always rechecks current authority.

Pending admission is state, not another delivery disposition. When evaluated
for delivery it uses the existing fired, coalesced, suppressed, or
capacity-refused pre-call set. Source, missed-fire, and overlap coalescing remain
distinct evidence-bearing mechanisms. Pending identity and ordering survive
crash recovery; revocation, expiry, supersession, narrowing, or policy revision
suppresses rather than reinterprets pending occurrences.

Rider R1: policy composition is fixed. Missed-fire evaluation first determines
which occurrences exist; every live or catch-up occurrence then passes through
overlap policy. Both stages' deterministic identity and order guarantees apply
across the seam, so crash recovery cannot bypass, double-admit, or reorder an
occurrence.

### Area 3 continuation / Decision 4C — resident-wide bounds and backpressure

-- Decision: 2026-07-21 trigger work is bounded by three named SPEC-local
constants: per-record pending occurrences, resident-wide pending trigger
occurrences, and resident-wide active trigger calls no greater than ordinary
active-call capacity. Admission order is missed-fire evaluation, overlap
policy, per-record pending capacity, resident-wide pending/active capacity, and
fresh current-authority/call checks before firing. No stage supplies another
stage's authority.

Pending admission becomes durable before scheduler visibility, never evicts
previously admitted work, and records stable identity, trigger revision,
represented set, admission sequence, policy evaluations, and queue reason.
Deterministic dequeue rechecks all current law. Restart reconstructs pending,
active, and terminal state from durable facts without assuming an unobserved
call completed. Failed append creates neither pending state nor call.

A new pending admission at either bound becomes terminal `capacity_refused`. A
directly fireable occurrence unable to reserve a resident active slot is also
terminally capacity-refused; there is no implicit second resident queue or
hidden retry. Only occurrences already admitted by overlap policy may wait for
an active slot. Trigger work cannot starve the writer, lifecycle control, status
reads, or ordinary resident admission.

Every eligible occurrence has durable pending/admitted, fired, coalesced,
suppressed, or capacity-refused evidence. A fired occurrence references its
existing `MctResult`; no post-call outcome set is duplicated.

Rider R1: terminal disposition gates re-evaluation. Missed-fire evaluation
excludes occurrences already durably skipped, suppressed, capacity-refused, or
fired with a terminal result. Terminality survives restart. Refusal cannot be
resurrected as a miss; only explicit policies may create a distinct later
governed occurrence.

The bounds/missed-fire named open question is closed. Item 4 is complete.

### Ratified rulings

1. `CallOrigin::TriggerFiring` is the single additive origin for temporal and
   event firings; it is local, may source one remote candidate, and never names
   a forwarded arrival.
2. Trigger-firing idempotency is scoped by governing trigger record and distinct
   deterministic occurrence identity.
3. Both observer shapes are lawful but exclusive per path: Child watch Toy or
   independently authorized Mother event-source adapter.
4. `WatchObservationScope` requires explicit canonical root/breadth, traversal,
   event classes, bounded batch, deterministic coalescing, validity, safe
   root-relative metadata, and per-scope sequence evidence.
5. Legacy `patina:watch/events@0.1.x` absolute-path semantics are rejected;
   byte-exact ABI compatibility requires equality with the root-relative slot,
   and successor contracts remove the field.
6. Child-observed delivery is an ordinary call (`WasmHost` for the fixture);
   Mother-observed delivery requires `TriggerFiring`. Both use durable
   batch/event/delivery evidence and actual causal lineage.
7. Delivery-specific disposition is pre-call only; post-call outcome always
   references the existing closed `MctResult` set.
8. Mother owns fire-at-T/fire-on-E mechanism; Child/application owns cadence,
   sync, polling, and debounce meaning beyond mechanical coalescing.
9. `missed_fire_policy` is `skip | coalesce_one | fire_late_bounded`, defaulting
   to skip, with deterministic crash-stable catch-up identities.
10. `overlap_policy` is `refuse | coalesce_one | queue_bounded`, defaulting to
    refuse, with one active call per trigger record.
11. Missed-fire evaluation precedes overlap evaluation, followed by per-record
    and resident-wide capacity and fresh current checks.
12. Three named SPEC-local limits bound per-record pending, resident-wide
    pending, and resident-wide active trigger calls. Capacity refusal is
    terminal, no-eviction, and creates no hidden retry.
13. Terminal dispositions survive restart and cannot be resurrected as misses.
14. Registry-sync initiation is deferred with four immediate interdicts and a
    coupled network-adapter gate.
