# MCT peer-Mother ontology elicitation

## Scope

Define the explicit relationships sovereign Mothers may instantiate with each other. Existing implementation is evidence that the operator may ratify or overturn. This session produces a standalone draft ontology and tend-pass inputs; it does not edit the product map, code, or ROADMAP.

## Binding constraints

- Nothing is ambient: each relationship is instantiated by an explicit, issued, scoped, time-bounded, revocable record.
- Deny is the passive default; capability advertisement is not a grant.
- Vision is the sharing boundary and is not reducible to tenant.
- Authority is inspectable data; each Mother remains sovereign and does not inherit another Mother's authority decisions.

## Decision 0 — Draft boundary

**Outcome:** Create `layer/allium/mct-peer-ontology.allium` as a standalone module. Cross-reference the product map's existing binding, hello, and ALPN constructs by name rather than duplicating them.

**Rationale:** Peer ontology is a coherent domain and should be independently checkable and reviewable. The reviewed tend pass, not this elicitation, will decide whether to retain the module with explicit product-map cross-references or merge its declarations into the product map.

**Implications:** The draft must not pre-commit to product-map insertion shape. The tend pass must choose integration form and prevent duplicated declarations.

## Decision log

### Agenda 1 — Relationship taxonomy

#### Decision 1.1 — Roles are current projections

**Outcome:** Caller, executor, and route-candidate are contextual roles derived from durable directional relationships. They are not independently issued statuses or grants.

**Rationale:** This applies the child-lifecycle separation of durable facts from derived usability to peers. A stored role grant would duplicate its source authority and could remain apparently valid after one source record changed; deriving roles from current records makes that disagreement unrepresentable and matches the two-phase route authority filter.

**Implications:**

- Every named role must be totally derivable from explicit, current records. If no total derivation exists, the ontology is missing a record type; an ambient status is not permitted.
- Every role derivation is normative spec content and must be stated as an invariant.
- Route-candidate derivation remains incomplete until Agenda 2 decides the publication term.
- The tend pass must preserve current-record evaluation and must not introduce stored caller, executor, or route-candidate grants.

#### Decision 1.2 — Taxonomy vocabulary and slots

**Outcome:** Ratify the proposed taxonomy with two added durable relationship slots and one lifecycle correction.

Current directional call relationships:

- `CallSubmissionAdmission`: the receiving Mother admits a peer to submit `mct/call/0` requests for evaluation. It is instantiated by her active, scoped, time-bounded `MctPeerBinding`; hello presents and negotiates that authority but does not grant call execution.
- `OutboundCallAuthorization`: a submitting Mother's authority to direct calls at a specific peer, issued by that peer. Agenda 5 decides its exact record set and interaction with `CallSubmissionAdmission`.
- `CapabilityPublication`: a record a Mother issues into a Vision advertising an operation. Agenda 2 decides its authority weight, commitment, and freshness contract.

Current contextual roles:

- `ImmediateCaller`: the Mother presenting a particular call to its receiver.
- `EligibleRouteCandidate`: a peer satisfying the complete current-record derivation for possible selection.
- `SelectedExecutor`: the selected candidate that remains authorized at execution revalidation.
- `CapabilityPublisher`: the Mother that issued the `CapabilityPublication` considered during candidacy.

Future directional relationship slots:

- `ThoughtExchangeAuthorization` over `mct/thought/0`; instantiating record open.
- `ObservationReplicationAuthorization` over `mct/observe/0`; instantiating record open.
- `FederationControlAuthorization` over `mct/federation/0`; instantiating record open.

A generic peer binding may admit ALPN negotiation for a future protocol but never defines that protocol's domain authority. Each future relationship requires its own explicit, scoped, expiring, revocable record before implementation.

**Conclusions and ending:** A call submitter may conclude only that it can present calls for evaluation while current admission holds. The receiver concludes only that the peer is eligible to submit, not that any call, route, child, toy, or effect is authorized. An existing admission ends by revocation, expiry, or supersession; hello denial means it never began.

**Rationale:** Directional call authority and capability publication need named record slots so every operational role can be totally derived without ambient or cached role state. `ImmediateCaller` deliberately postpones the end-to-end accountability decision.

**Implications:** Agenda 2 must define `CapabilityPublication`; Agenda 5 must define `OutboundCallAuthorization` and bilateral state derivations. Future protocol implementation is blocked on defining each named authorization record.

_Status: complete._

### Agenda 2 — Publication commitment (D1)

#### Decision 2.1 — Revocable local execution offer

**Outcome:** `CapabilityPublication` is a revocable local execution offer. While current, it says the publisher offers to evaluate conforming calls for local execution of the advertised operation. It does not guarantee success, reserve capacity, or displace per-call current-authority, readiness, data-policy, and availability evaluation.

The offer has three normative riders:

1. **Capability, not authority:** publication contributes the capability term to `EligibleRouteCandidate`. It is necessary but never sufficient. Directional authority records independently determine whether the caller may send there.
2. **Honest local offer:** a Mother publishes only operations she would evaluate for local execution. Publishing an operation she cannot or will not locally run is a specification violation. Publication never advertises brokerage.
3. **Freshness as currency:** publication remains an offer only within its freshness window. Expiry lapses the offer without fault; consumers remove the candidate. Ceasing republication is passive withdrawal bounded by that window.

**Rationale:** A guaranteed execution promise conflicts with sovereign current-authority revalidation and would require reservation machinery. Historical evidence is too weak for actively maintained offers and would require a duplicate candidacy record contrary to Decision 1.1.

**Implications:**

- The product map must state that publication is an execution offer and contributes capability, never grant authority.
- `CapabilityPublisher` derives from issuance of a current `CapabilityPublication`.
- `EligibleRouteCandidate` must conjunct current publication with the directional authority records settled in Agenda 5.
- Brokerage publication is excluded. Any future brokering model requires a distinct ontology and authority record rather than overloading `CapabilityPublication`.
- The current local ready/approved/assigned/same-Vision publication filter is evidence aligned with the honesty obligation.

**Open:** Every publication must remain explicitly revocable under the binding constraints, but the mechanism and consumer-visible semantics of revocation beyond bounded non-republication are deferred unless another agenda decision requires them.

_Status: complete._

### Agenda 3 — Terminal arrivals and origin (D2 + A1)

#### Decision 3.1 — Peer call submission proposes local execution

**Outcome:** Terminality derives from `mct/call/0` protocol semantics and its execution-offer relationship, not from `CallOrigin`. `CallSubmissionAdmission` licenses a peer to submit a call for local execution at the receiving Mother: the submission means, “I propose you as the local executor of this call.” The receiver therefore evaluates her local candidates because that is the question submitted.

`CapabilityPublication` is how a rational peer chooses a receiver, but every admitted `mct/call/0` submission has propose-local-execution meaning whether it references an offer or arrives blind. A publication reference is not required for terminality.

**A1 disposition:** `OriginIsForObservationNotPermission` stands unamended. The current Iroh-origin gate is the adapter's mechanical observation/dispatch witness of which protocol question arrived; origin creates and removes no authority. Option B was rejected because it elevates mechanism into origin-specific authority. Option C was rejected because a `PeerExecutionSubmission` record would duplicate facts totally derivable from an admitted protocol arrival. A richer future submission record is justified only when it carries non-derivable content such as end-to-end caller identity.

**Single-hop law:** `mct/call/0` is permanently terminal at its receiving Mother. Multi-hop is not a relaxation of this rule. It requires a new brokered-submission relationship with its own protocol semantics, end-to-end caller identity, and explicit transitive-routing policy.

**Rationale:** The protocol's domain question explains local-only evaluation without granting special authority to an adapter-origin enum or storing redundant arrival authority. It preserves both adapter-neutral authority and sovereign per-call evaluation.

**Implications:**

- The product map must define `mct/call/0` as a proposal that the receiver execute locally and state that arrivals cannot source another peer candidate.
- `OriginIsForObservationNotPermission` remains unchanged; tend should describe the current origin gate as protocol dispatch, not authority.
- The ROADMAP's end-to-end identity and transitive-policy prerequisites remain consistent, but a future tend pass should frame multi-hop as a distinct brokered relationship rather than an extension of `mct/call/0`.
- D2 is ratified as protocol law. A1 is resolved without weakening its invariant.

**Follow-on:** A future call envelope may carry the selected `CapabilityPublication` reference so route audit can prove which offer informed selection. This is an audit enhancement, not authority and not a prerequisite for terminality.

_Status: complete._

### Agenda 4 — Accountability model (D3)

#### Decision 4.1 — Permanent per-hop vouching for `mct/call/0`

**Outcome:** Per-hop vouching is the permanent trust algebra of `mct/call/0`. The receiving Mother authorizes and records only the admitted `ImmediateCaller`; upstream identities do not cross this protocol. Landed caller rewriting is ratified, not a temporary limitation.

The vouching contract has three normative riders:

1. **Submission is the peer's act:** by submitting, the `ImmediateCaller` asserts that her own authority evaluation authorized the submission. The receiver attributes misuse to that Mother and may respond through bilateral authority, including revocation. No upstream-person or upstream-node assertion is made to the receiver.
2. **Bilateral audit reconstruction:** cross-node audit joins each Mother's ledger using preserved call and trace identifiers. Each Mother discloses only under her own authority; upstream identity remains in the ledger where it was verified. Future authorized sharing uses `ObservationReplicationAuthorization` over `mct/observe/0`, not identity claims embedded in calls.
3. **Clean envelope by design:** `mct/call/0` carries no originator-identity field. Adding one is not an enhancement to this protocol. Authenticated end-to-end identity and any executor-held originator grant belong to the distinct future brokered-submission relationship.

**Rationale:** The receiver can verify the peer she admitted, but cannot treat that peer's assertions about upstream identities as her own authority. Per-hop vouching preserves sovereignty and keeps each ledger's facts within the authority that verified them.

**Implications:**

- The product map must state immediate-caller attribution and per-hop vouching explicitly.
- Call and trace identifiers form the cross-ledger correlation contract; identity does not.
- The future observation-replication ontology must define authorized ledger-slice exchange and preserve each ledger's disclosure policy.
- Any future brokered relationship requires authenticated end-to-end identity plus executor-held authority; it must not mutate `mct/call/0` semantics.
- D3 is resolved by ratifying landed caller rewriting.

_Status: complete._

### Agenda 5 — Bilateral relationship (D4)

#### Decision 5.1 — Mutual admission gates derived routing

**Outcome:** Mutual admission is an intentional, permanent prerequisite for executable resident peer routing. Routing from A to B exposes A's data and routing fabric to B and imports B's result. It therefore requires two sovereign consents:

- B's admission of A, possessed by A as `OutboundCallAuthorization`, is B's consent to ingress.
- A's admission of B is A's own peer-trust, egress, and data-placement authority for treating B as a destination and result source.

`OutboundCallAuthorization` is possession of the peer-issued `CallSubmissionAdmission`, not a third authority-record type.

**Complete `EligibleRouteCandidate` derivation:** B is eligible at A for operation X if and only if every current per-call term holds:

1. A actively admits B under current Vision, policy revision, and time scope.
2. B actively admits A, witnessed by B's current call-ALPN-scoped outbound proof held by A.
3. B has a fresh `CapabilityPublication` offering X for local execution.
4. All three records agree on Vision.
5. A has current ticket reachability evidence for B.

No role or routable flag stores this conjunction.

**Independent ending:** Either directional admission may be revoked, expire, or be superseded independently. Loss of either removes B from candidacy at A at the next current-record evaluation. A one-way state remains useful for serving submissions in the direction covered by its issuer, but does not establish derived outbound candidacy in the reverse direction.

**Operator-pointed distinction:** Mutual admission gates derived resident route candidacy. An operator explicitly directing a call at a peer exercises local authority for that individual egress decision rather than asking the candidate derivation to choose. Receiver admission of the submitting Mother and all receiver-side per-call authority remain mandatory.

**Rationale:** Outbound-only routing would let B write itself into A's routing fabric by issuing ingress consent and publishing a surface, without any A-issued authority record. Mutual admission preserves deny-by-default, data-placement authority, and publication's non-grant status.

**Implications:**

- The product map must model the bilateral relationship as two independently issued `MctPeerBinding` records and derive pair states rather than storing them.
- State witnesses are: stranger = neither record current; inbound-admitted = local admission of peer current; outbound-authorized = peer admission of local Mother current; mutually-admitted = both current; routable-for-operation = mutual plus fresh publication, Vision agreement, and ticket.
- The state space is a reversible two-direction lattice, not a mandatory linear transition order.
- The tend pass must state the operator-pointed per-call egress distinction without weakening receiver admission or effect authority.
- D4 is resolved by ratifying the landed bilateral route filter as two-sovereign consent.

_Status: complete._

## Finding dispositions

- **A1:** resolved. `OriginIsForObservationNotPermission` stands; terminality derives from the peer call protocol question, not origin authority.
- **D1:** ratified and defined. Publication is a fresh, honest, revocable local execution offer contributing capability but no grant.
- **D2:** ratified as permanent `mct/call/0` terminality.
- **D3:** ratified as permanent per-hop vouching and immediate-caller rewriting.
- **D4:** ratified as bilateral two-sovereign consent for derived executable routing.

## Open questions

1. **Publication revocation:** What explicit revocation record or signal complements bounded passive withdrawal, and when do consumers treat it as effective?
2. **Publication freshness policy:** Is the current 300-second window fixed protocol law, issuer-declared, negotiated, or local consumer policy?
3. **Thought exchange:** Which issued record instantiates `ThoughtExchangeAuthorization`, and what may each side conclude?
4. **Observation replication:** Which record scopes replicated ledger slices, audience, retention, onward disclosure, and revocation?
5. **Federation control:** Which record scopes control actions without delegating one Mother's sovereignty to another?
6. **Brokered submission:** What relationship record and protocol carry verifiable end-to-end identity and transitive-routing authority?
7. **Route-audit reference:** Should a future call envelope carry the `CapabilityPublication` reference that informed selection?
